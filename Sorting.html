Sorting Algorithms

Efficient ways to organize data for better performance and searchability.

Bubble Sort

Time: \(O(n^2)\)

A straightforward comparison sort that repeatedly steps through the list, compares adjacent elements, and swaps them if they are in the wrong order. Best for educational purposes or nearly-sorted data.

Quick Sort

Avg: \(O(n \log n)\)

A high-performance divide-and-conquer algorithm. It picks a 'pivot' element and partitions the array into two sub-arrays, sorting them recursively. It is the standard for most modern sorting libraries.
