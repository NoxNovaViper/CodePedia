Bubble Sort

Space: \(O(1)\) Time: \(O(n^2)\)

The simplest comparison-based algorithm. It operates by repeatedly swapping adjacent elements that are out of sequence. While inefficient for large datasets, its in-place nature makes it memory efficient.

Quick Sort

Space: \(O(\log n)\) Avg: \(O(n \log n)\)

A highly efficient Divide and Conquer pattern. By selecting a 'pivot' and partitioning the array, it reduces the sorting problem into smaller sub-problems. It is the backbone of most production-level sorting functions.
